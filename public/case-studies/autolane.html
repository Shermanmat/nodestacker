USV, General Catalyst, Matrix, Precursor, SignalFire, HF0, Long Journey, Weekend Fund — a dozen of the sharpest minds in early-stage took the meeting. That's the crew you want kicking the tires on a big thesis before you go raise.

The challenge

Autolane is a big, early thesis: infrastructure to coordinate the flood of autonomous vehicles arriving at private property — the malls, retailers, and restaurants where AVs will pick up, drop off, and deliver. Ten million AVs are projected at American curbs by 2030. Someone has to be the air traffic control.

At that stage, what Ben needed was a sharp, forwardable story and warm intros to the right investors — and someone who believed enough to open the door.

What MatCap did

  • Backed it early. One of the first to hear the idea and believe in it — before the round, before the momentum.
  • Sharpened the messaging. Helped tighten how Autolane's story lands with investors — the pitch, not the product.
  • Made 12 warm VC intros to a top-tier crew — USV (two meetings), Niko Bonatsos, Matrix, Precursor, SignalFire, HF0, Long Journey, Weekend Fund, Boost VC, Chapter One's Jeff Morris Jr., and operator angels.

The outcome

  • $7.4M seed, led by Draper and Hyperplane — sourced separately, not through MatCap.
  • A tighter story, and 12 warm intros to a dozen of the sharpest early-stage investors in the game.
  • Our fingerprints are on the early conviction, the messaging, and the intros — not the lead check. That's the honest version, and we're proud of it.
