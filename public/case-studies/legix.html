The challenge

This founder and Mat met in 2019 — friendship first, business much later. The founder iterated through several ideas across six years (consumer services, vertical SaaS) before landing on a thesis that clicked. Through it all, the relationship deepened, but mixing money into a friendship took trust to build.

When the right idea finally landed and a real round needed to come together, the question was whether years of casual support could convert into high-conviction, targeted fundraising — and survive the inevitable tight moments.

What MatCap did

  • Six years of consistent show-up — events, intros, advice — long before there was equity in the room.
  • Early casual intros that brought in roughly $100K in initial capital.
  • When the larger round opened: high-conviction, targeted outreach into MatCap's network. 67 intro requests, hand-picked.
  • Connected the founder with key investors including Outlander Labs (AJ Smith), Forum Ventures (Nick Saah), Incisive VC (direct intro), and Untapped VC as the round lead.
  • Stayed in the round through the tight moments — the moments rounds either close or fall apart.

The outcome

  • $1.5M round closed led by Untapped VC.
  • Three wired checks via MatCap intros — Outlander Labs and Forum Ventures (platform), plus Incisive VC (direct intro from Mat outside the platform flow).
  • Tier 1 firms (Benchmark, Greylock, Menlo, Hustle Fund, Homebrew, Bloomberg Beta, South Park Commons) took the meeting; none led.
  • Founder built out a strong senior team and is positioned to scale.
  • A six-year relationship-first arc — the kind that doesn't show up in transactional networks.
