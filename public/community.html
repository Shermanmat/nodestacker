WHY THIS EXISTS

Most founder communities are noise.

If you're building a venture-scale company and you want a real peer group instead of a feed — this is the room.

THE RHYTHM

The cadence is the product.

Same five rituals at both tiers. Showing up is the membership.

  • WEEKLY
    Async progress updates. Every Friday, one prompt lands: what did you get done last week? A few sentences, no more. Keeps you accountable, and keeps the MatCap team in the loop on how you're progressing.
    FRIDAY · 9:00 AM
    What did you get done last week?
    From MatCap · Reply with a few sentences
  • BIWEEKLY
    Workshops. A member or invited operator teaches what's actually working. Hiring, pricing, fundraising mechanics, AI workflows, GTM — the unglamorous stuff that moves the number.
  • MONTHLY
    Social events. Poker nights, dinners, go-kart racing. Anchored locally per chapter. Built for bonding — and the conversations that come with it.
  • QUARTERLY
    Retreats. A few days off the grind with the rest of the founders. Come for the day or stay the night — a chance to get closer to the community and the MatCap team.
  • ANNUAL
    MatCap Community Conference. A full-send annual conference — speakers, a curated room, and the full network in one place. We go all out to showcase the founders in the MatCap ecosystem.
WHY THE FILTER

Outside a Tier 1 hub, the signal around you is lower. We raise it by raising the bar on who gets in.

In SF or New York, signal density is automatic. The founder next to you at the coffee shop is also building a venture-scale company. Benchmarks are visible. Standards are obvious because they're everywhere.

Outside those hubs, you have to work to find your peers — and to know whether what you're building actually clears the bar. We solve that by being aggressive about who gets in. Every person in the room earns their seat.
