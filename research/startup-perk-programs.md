# NodeStacker Perk Program — Distribution-Partner Research

**Goal:** Build a robust perk program where NodeStacker's founder users redeem free credits and discounts from dozens of relevant vendors — with NodeStacker acting as an **official distribution partner** (the way accelerators, VCs, and marketplaces like Secret or AngelList distribute deals), not just a link list.

**Prepared:** 2026-07-11 · **Method:** multi-source web research with adversarial fact-checking (23 of 24 extracted claims confirmed; 1 refuted). See [Verification & Caveats](#verification--caveats).

---

## TL;DR — the one decision that matters

**Don't chase 50 individual partnerships first. Chase one aggregator.**

Partnering with a single perk *aggregator* exposes **hundreds of vendor deals at once** through one relationship, versus negotiating ~50 programs individually (most of which gate their best credits behind VC/accelerator accreditation NodeStacker doesn't have yet). The aggregator route is the fastest path to a "dozens of perks" catalog, and it can be **white-labeled to live inside NodeStacker's own product**.

## How to read this doc

**Verification legend** — the research verified the aggregator + a handful of direct programs in depth; the majority of individually-named vendor programs were **not** individually confirmed to run an *open third-party distribution program*. Each row is tagged:

- ✅ **Verified** — distribution-partner path confirmed in this research with a source.
- 🟡 **Partner path likely / documented elsewhere** — program is well-known; partner/community route is documented but was not deep-verified here. Reconfirm before outreach.
- ⚠️ **Founder-facing only / unconfirmed** — the credit exists, but no open partner-distribution program was found. Route = direct BD outreach or a warm intro.

**Difficulty tier:**
- **Easy** — self-serve or application-based marketplace/community partner; no accreditation.
- **Moderate** — application + fit review; community orgs accepted.
- **Hard** — requires accreditation as an accelerator/VC/incubator, or a warm introduction.

**On named contacts:** the research found **no publicly-listed individual BD contacts** for any of these programs — only role emails and application forms. This doc does **not** invent names or email addresses. Where only a form exists, that is stated. To find a named human, the reliable method is LinkedIn: search *"Head of Startups"* / *"Startup Partnerships"* / *"Ecosystem Partnerships"* at the target company and request a warm intro (NodeStacker's relationship-building product is itself the intro engine).

## Recommended 90-day rollout

1. **Weeks 1–2 — Land the anchor aggregator.** Apply to **Secret** ([partners-program](https://www.joinsecret.com/partners-program) / applications@joinsecret.com). Ask specifically about the **white-label iFrame embed** so the perk marketplace can render inside NodeStacker. This single step gives founders 600+ deals.
2. **Weeks 2–3 — Add a second catalog.** Self-serve signup with **FounderPass** ([partner-signup](https://www.founderpass.com/partner-signup)) for catalog diversity and better commission economics.
3. **Weeks 3–5 — Land the flagship direct programs** that accept communities: **Datadog** (ddfspartners@datadoghq.com), **HubSpot** ([partners](https://www.hubspot.com/startups/partners)), **Notion** ([form](https://www.notion.com/help/notion-for-startups)), **Microsoft Founders Hub**. These are the logos founders recognize.
4. **Weeks 5–8 — Broaden reach.** Direct outreach to **F6S**, **Startups.com Perks**, **StartupStash**, **Startup Science**; open **co-marketing/referral** talks with **Mercury / Brex / Ramp**.
5. **Weeks 8–12 — Go for the marquee credit.** Apply to become an **AWS Activate Provider** once NodeStacker can demonstrate a real, active founder base — this is the accreditation-gated prize worth up to $100k–$300k per founder.

**Outreach method for named humans:** none of these programs publish an individual BD contact. Use the role emails/forms above to start, and in parallel use LinkedIn to find each company's *"Head of Startups Program,"* *"Startup Partnerships,"* or *"Ecosystem Partnerships"* lead and request a warm intro. (NodeStacker's own relationship-graph product is the ideal tool for sourcing those intros.)

## Verification & Caveats

- **What was deep-verified (✅):** the aggregator thesis and Secret's Partners Program + white-label embed + applications@joinsecret.com; FounderPass partner signup + $4M/350+ deals; Datadog referral-partner-only model + ddfspartners@datadoghq.com + startups@datadoghq.com; AWS Activate Providers model; HubSpot & Notion & Microsoft community-partner eligibility; fintech accounts unlocking 50–100+ perks each. (23 of 24 extracted claims confirmed by 3-vote adversarial check.)
- **What was NOT individually verified (🟡/⚠️):** whether *each* named vendor (Snowflake, Cloudflare, Twilio/Segment, Intercom, Zendesk, Figma, Retool, Webflow, Zoom, Atlassian, GitLab, Carta, OpenAI/Anthropic, etc.) runs an **open third-party distribution program** for a small community, and their exact eligibility bars. Treat those rows as leads to confirm, not commitments.
- **No named individuals were found publicly** for any program — only role emails and forms. This doc deliberately does not invent names or addresses.
- **Figures drift.** Credit amounts and deal counts change often (AWS Activate is cited at $100k / $200k / $300k-for-AI across 2026 sources; Secret's catalog as 573 / 600+ / 1,000+; FounderPass grew $3M→$4M). Reconfirm current numbers before you publish them to founders or use them in outreach.
- **One claim was refuted** and excluded: that a pre-seed startup can "stack ~$30k in 60 days" with no VC/accelerator/referral — the adversarial check killed it 0–3.
- **Source-quality note:** part of the broad program-count/fintech-perk landscape traces to a single promotional blog; those specifics were cross-checked against primary vendor pages, but the "$500k on the table" framing is marketing.
